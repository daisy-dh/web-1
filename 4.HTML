    Holiday in Korea

    South Korea’s daily COVID-19 cases bounced back to a three-digit figure Wednesday after six days of double-digit gains , as the country might be beginning to see the impact of last week’s extended Chuseok holiday on the spread of the coronavirus.
    Korea added 114 new COVID-19 cases, including 94 locally transmitted, according to the Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency.

    The tally marks a jump from 75 cases posted a day earlier. The country’s daily infections had hovered around 70 for six consecutive days until Tuesday. There have been concerns for a possible spike in new infections after the extended Chuseok holiday, which ran from Sept. 30 to Sunday, as millions of people moved across the country for family visits or vacations.
